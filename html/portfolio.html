KombOver: Efficient k-core and K-truss based characterization of chronic disease impact on the human gut microbiome

KombOver (KO) is an updated version of the computational biology tool KOMB developed at the Treangen Lab. KOMB is a metagenomic data (a bunch of small sections of the genomes of many different organisms from a genetic hotspot, such as the human gut) processing tool with a command-line interface. It utilizes the De Bruijn graph representation of contiguous sequences extracted from a set of reads, and executes different graph mining methods on the final structure to make valid and useful conclusions. Over the summer, together with N. Sapoval (a PhD student at the Treangen Lab) and mentored by our PI, we focused on improving this tool by improving the pipeline (and as such, running speed), adding additional functionalities, and running it on new datasets. In the end, we submitted a paper summarizing our findings to the Pacific Symposium of Biocomputing, and it was accepted!

Authdaddy

During the PennApps XXIV hackathon (hosted by the University of Pennsylvania), along with 3 other students, we developed an app that serves as an alternative to 2-factor authentication. Here is the official description:

"AuthDaddy is a groundbreaking Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) solution developed during PennApps XXIV (2023). Unlike traditional 2FA systems that rely on multiple electronic devices and complex network infrastructure, AuthDaddy simplifies the authentication process by harnessing a user's unique biometric typing patterns. This innovative approach not only enhances security but also addresses critical environmental concerns associated with conventional 2FA methods."

We used standard front-end technologies (HTML, JavaScript, CSS, Jekyll), along with Python and MATLAB for the back-end ML/statistical modeling and data-processing. I was personally responsible for developing a signifcant amount of the modeling, data analysis, and machine learning algorithms. We won the track "Most Creative Use of GitHub."

Precinct Ranking System

An analysis of the nonprofit BakerRipley's voting dataset created during the Rice Datathon 2023. Along with 3 other team members, we developed methods to gauge effectiveness of attempts at increasing voting participation. I personally used the Sci-kit Learn library in Python, and different ML methods such as LASSO regression and logistic regression (support vector machines) to calculate probability of voting of a given person based on identifying parameters such as age, precinct of residence, gender, etc.
